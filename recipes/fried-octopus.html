Fried Octopus Recipe

Years ago I made my first ever fried octopus using this recipe from Maangchi. The texture was out of this world. The taste magnificent. I even shared it with my family and the children loved it.

Today I am going to list the ingredients and the instructions to make that delicious dish. I hope you try this and if you do like it please make sure to share it with others by going directly to the original website.

Ingredients

  • 1 package (1 1/2 pounds) of thawed octopus
  • 2 tablespoons flour (or salt)
  • 7 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 teaspoons minced ginger
  • 1 Korean spicy green chili pepper (jalapeno), chopped
  • 4 ounces onion, chopped
  • 1 large Korean green onion, chopped
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil

For seasonings:

  • 1/4 cup Korean hot pepper flakes
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon potato starch (or corn starch)
  • 1/4 cup rice syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

Steps

Prep the Octopus:

  1. Cut open the head and remove the beaks and intestines
  2. Put into a large bowl . Add flour and scrub vigorously 2 minutes or until it foams.
  3. Rinse in cold running water, until its no longer slippery anymore. Drain.
  4. Cut the heads and arms into bite size pieces. Set aside.

Make seasoning sauce:

  1. Combine all the seasoning ingrediants (hot pepper flakes, soy sauce, sugar, potato starch, rice syrup and ground black pepper) in a small bowl
  2. Mix well with a spoon.

Stir-fry

  1. Add oil to pan and put it on high heat
  2. Stir garlic, ginger, green chili pepper, onion, and green onion for about 3 minutes until the garlic turns a little brown and the onion translucent.
  3. Add the octopus and the seasoning sauce. Stir for 2 to 3 minutes.
  4. Remove from the heat and stir in sesame oil. Put on a serving dish with the side. Rice was recommended.
